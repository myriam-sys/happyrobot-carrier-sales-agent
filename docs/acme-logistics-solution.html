The Problem

Your dispatchers are doing work a machine should do

Every day, carriers call your team asking the same questions: what loads are available, what equipment types do you need, what rate are you offering? Your dispatchers check the loadboard, quote a number, and either book the load or lose the carrier to the next call in the queue.

That loop runs dozens to hundreds of times per day. It requires no judgment. It pulls your best people away from the negotiations that do require judgment, and it stops entirely when your office closes.

The average freight brokerage loses 15–25% of after-hours carrier inquiries because no one is available to take the call. Those are covered loads that never get covered.
What We Built

A live system — not a prototype

The solution running today for Acme Logistics consists of a HappyRobot AI voice agent connected to a dedicated backend API. When a carrier calls in, the agent handles the entire conversation: it does not route to a human unless it encounters something it is not configured to handle.

  • 1
    Load matching. The agent queries available loads in real time, filtered by the carrier's equipment type and where they are running. It presents specific options — not a generic "we have loads available."
  • 2
    Carrier verification. Before any rate is discussed, the agent checks the carrier's MC number against FMCSA federal records and confirms active operating authority and insurance. Ineligible carriers are declined politely and immediately, with no dispatcher time spent.
  • 3
    Rate negotiation. The agent opens at your configured target rate and follows your negotiation strategy — how many rounds, how much concession, when to hold firm. Every interaction enforces your rate discipline consistently.
  • 4
    Automatic logging. Every call is recorded with outcome, final rate, number of rounds, call duration, and carrier sentiment. Your team has a complete audit trail without listening to a single recording.

What Your Team Sees

A real-time view of every carrier interaction

Your operations team does not need to listen to calls or pull reports to know how the day is going. The live dashboard surfaces the numbers that matter — booking rate, average rate achieved, carrier sentiment, and which lanes are generating the most activity — updated continuously as calls come in.

The call log at the bottom of the dashboard shows every recent interaction: who called, which load they asked about, what rate was agreed (or why the call did not book), and how long the conversation lasted. Exceptions stand out immediately.
